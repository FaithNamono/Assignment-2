CO-CURRICULAR ACTIVITIES

SPORTS

Sports are physical activities that involve skill, competition, and often, teamwork. They can be played individually or in teams, and they usually have specific rules and objectives. Sports are a way to stay physically active, entertain, and build camaraderie.

TYPES OF SPORTS

Ball Sports: Involving a ball or puck.

Racquet Sports: Involving a racquet and a ball or shuttlecock.

Combat Sports: Involving physical contact and fighting techniques.

Endurance Sports: Focusing on stamina and physical endurance.

Gymnastics: Involving physical strength, flexibility, and coordination

Water Sports: Performed in or on water (e.g., swimming, sailing, surfing)

Air Sports: Performed in the air (e.g.skydiving, paragliding)

Motor Sports: Involving motorized vehicles (e.g car racing, motorcycle racing)

OTHER SPORTS

Football is a well-established and popular sport, played both professionally and recreationally around the world.

Netball is also a recognized sport, with organized leagues and competitions.

BENEFITS OF SPORTS

Physical Benefits:

Improved Cardiovascular Health. Enhanced Muscle Strength and Endurance. Weight Management. Increased Bone Density. Improved Flexibility and Balance.

Mental Benefits:

Stress Relief. Improved Sleep. Increased Self-Esteem and Confidence. Enhanced Cognitive Function. Improved Concentration and Focus.

Social Benefits:

Teamwork and Collaboration. Social Connections. Leadership and Communication Skills. Respect for Others. Sense of Belonging.

Other Benefits:

Discipline and Time Management. Goal Setting and Achievement. Resilience and Perseverance.

Overall, sports offer a multitude of benefits that can positively impact your physical, mental, and social well-being. Whether you choose a team sport, individual activity, or something completely new, finding an activity you enjoy can lead to a healthier and happier life.
